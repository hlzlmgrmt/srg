About Equipment

Equipment is a fundermental part of any runners arsenal, as most of their hard-earned money is going to be spent on new tools, weapons, and tech to survive the next run.

Item Qualities

Some equipment features special qualities that add variety and depth to the massive array of weapons, armor, and items that exist in the Sixth World. Some qualities are inherent so certain items, in order to differentiate them from another outside of simple damage numbers, while others can be applied through modifications to specifically tailor a piece of equipment to someones liking.

Item qualities are generally either passive or active. Passive qualities are always "on" and require no activation on the part of the user. Active qualities must be triggered by the user, often by spending one or more a to activate the effect.

Item qualities often have a number associated with them. This is their rating. Ratings affect qualities in different ways, depending on the quality in question.

Active qualities require aa to activate unless otherwise stated in their description.

Accurate (Passive)

Accurate weapons are easier to aim or wield, whether through design or technology, and have an inherit advantage as a consequence of that. For each rank of this quality, the attacker adds j to their combat checks while using this weapon.

Blast (Active)

The weapon has a large spread, an explosive blast, or a similar area of effect, like a detonated grenade or a warhead fired from a missile launcher. If the attack is successful and Blast activates, each character (friend or foe) engaged with the original target suffers a hit dealing damage equal to the Blast quality’s rating, plus damage equal to the total s scored on the check.

In a relatively small and enclosed area, the Game Master might decide that everyone in the room suffers damage.

If the Blast quality doesn’t activate, the ordnance still detonates, but bad luck or poor aim on the part of the firer (or quick reactions on the part of the targets) means the explosion may not catch anyone else in its radius. However, the user may also trigger Blast if the attack misses by spending aaa. In this case, the original target and every target engaged with the original target suffers a hit dealing damage equal to the Blast rating of the weapon.

Breach (Passive)

Weapons with Breach burn through the toughest armor; they are often heavy weapons or weapons mounted on some sort of vehicle.

Hits from weapons with the Breach quality ignore one point of vehicle armor for every rating of Breach (meaning they also ignore 10 soak for every rating of Breach).

Burn (Active)

Weapons with Burn inflict damage over time. When Burn is triggered, one target hit by the attack continues to suffer the weapon’s base damage each round for a number of rounds equal to the weapon’s Burn rating. Apply damage at the start of each of the target’s turns. If multiple targets suffer hits from a weapon with Burn, the quality may be triggered multiple times, affecting a different target each time.

A victim might be able to stop the damage by performing an action to roll around and make a Coordination check. The difficulty is Average (kk) on hard surfaces such as the floor of a building, or Easy (k) on grass or soft ground. Jumping into a body of water stops the damage immediately. Both situations assume the flame is from actual combustion rather than a chemical reaction. With the latter, there is usually little the victim can do.

Concealable (Passive)

Equipment with Concealable are harder to spot and to find, by having a combination of a particular small profile, scanner-resistant materials or an unusual form. For each rank of this quality, anyone attempting to find the item, including through scanners and the Matrix, adds j to checks to find the item.

Concussive (Active)

The weapon’s attack can leave the target shell-shocked from mighty blows or punishing shock waves, unable to perform any but the most basic actions. When Concussive is triggered, one target hit by the attack is staggered for a number of rounds equal to the weapon’s Concussive rating. A staggered target cannot perform actions. If multiple targets suffer hits from a weapon with Concussive, the quality may be triggered multiple times, affecting a different target each time.

Cumbersome (Passive)

A Cumbersome weapon is large, unwieldy, awkward, or heavy. To wield a Cumbersome weapon properly, the character needs a Brawn characteristic equal to or greater than the weapon’s Cumbersome rating. For each point of Brawn by which the character is deficient, they must increase the difficulty of all checks made while using the weapon by one.

Defensive (Passive)

An item with the Defensive quality increases the user’s melee defense by its Defensive rating.

Deflective (Passive)

An item with the Deflective quality increases the user’s ranged defense by its Deflective rating.

Disorient (Active)

A weapon with Disorient can daze an opponent. When Disorient is triggered, one target hit by the attack is disoriented for a number of rounds equal to the weapon’s Disorient rating. A disoriented target adds j to all skill checks they perform. If multiple targets suffer hits from a weapon with Disorient, the quality may be triggered multiple times, affecting a different target each time.

Ensnare (Active)

A weapon with Ensnare binds a foe and restricts their movements. When Ensnare is triggered, one target hit by the attack becomes immobilized. for a number of rounds equal to the weapon’s Ensnare rating. An immobilized target cannot perform maneuvers. If multiple targets suffer hits from a weapon with Ensnare, the quality may be triggered multiple times, affecting a different target each time.

Full-Auto (Active)

A weapon with Full-Auto can be set to shoot in rapid succession and potentially spray an area with bolts, flechettes, slugs, or other types of projectiles. The advantage in using Full-Auto is that it has the chance to hit multiple targets or to hit a single target multiple times.

As attacking with a weapon in Full-Auto is generally less accurate, the attacker must increase the difficulty of the combat check by k. The user may choose not to use the Full-Auto quality on a weapon; in this case, they cannot trigger the quality but also do not suffer the aforementioned penalty.

If the attack hits, the attacker can trigger Full-Auto by spending aa. Full-Auto can be triggered multiple times. Each time the attacker triggers Full-Auto it deals an additional hit to the target. Each of these counts as an additional hit from that weapon, and each hit deals base damage plus the number of s on the check.

These additional hits can be allocated to the original target, or to other targets within range of the weapon. If the attacker wishes to hit multiple targets, they must decide to do so before making the check. Furthermore, if they wish to hit multiple targets, their initial target must always be the target with the highest difficulty and highest defense (if this is two separate targets, the GM chooses which is the initial target). The initial hit must always be against the initial target. Subsequent hits generated can be allocated to any of the other designated targets.

Full-Auto weapons can also activate one Critical Injury for each hit generated on the attack, per the normal rules; the Critical Injury must be applied to the target of the specific hit.

Guided (Active)

A weapon with the Guided quality can track opponents. The Guided quality can only be triggered if an attack misses. If Guided is triggered, the controlling character may make a combat check at the end of the round as an out-of-turn incidental. The difficulty of this combat check is Average (kk); instead of building the ability of the pool normally, add k equal to the weapon’s Guided rating. If the check is successful, the weapon strikes the target, and the attack is resolved normally.

Guided requires aaa to activate, unless otherwise specified in the weapon’s description. The Guided quality can activate on any subsequent combat check it makes, representing the projectile continuing to track the target.

Hardened (Passive)

Weapons or items with the Hardened quality are immune to the Sunder quality. Armor with the Hardened quality make the wearer’s soak immune to the Pierce and Breach qualities.

Inaccurate (Passive)

Inaccurate weapons are less likely to be accurate or precise. When making an attack with an Inaccurate weapon, add j to the check equal to the Inaccurate rating.

Inferior (Passive)

An Inferior item is a lackluster example of its kind, representing shoddy and poor craftsmanship. An Inferior item generates automatic h on all checks related to its use.

Ion (Passive)

An weapon with the Ion quality is especially destructive towards electronic equipment, including personal devices, drones, vehicles and even personas. Instead of wounds, weapons with the Ion quality deal System Strain equal to the weapons' damage, plus any uncancelled s. If the target of the attack does not have a System Strain monitor, it instead gets damaged by one step, plus an additional step for each uncancelled a generated by the attack, potentially breaking it in a single attack.

Unless otherwise specified, weapons with the Ion quality generally ignore the targets' soak and armor ratings; instead, the suffered System Strain is reduced by target's Firewall rating.

Knockdown (Active)

When Knockdown is triggered, one target hit by the attack is knocked prone. If multiple targets suffer hits from a weapon with Knockdown, the quality may be triggered multiple times, affecting a different target each time.

Unless specified otherwise, Knockdown requires aa to trigger, plus one additional a per silhouette of the target beyond 1.

Limited Ammo (Passive)

Some weapons fire particularly large or complex projectiles that cost lots of money. Other weapons are expendable weapons like grenades that, once used, are destroyed. A weapon with the Limited Ammo quality may be used to make a number of attacks equal to its Limited Ammo rating before it must be reloaded with a maneuver. In addition, each shot expends one of a limited number of rounds of ammo; more ammo must be purchased or obtained before anyone fires the weapon again. This also applies to grenades and other “one-use” weapons that have the Limited Ammo 1 quality (here, your character is not “reloading” the grenade, but drawing another to use—mechanically, they are equivalent).

Linked (Active)

Some weapons are designed to fire together at the same target (turrets housing multiple guns are a good example of this). When a character fires a linked weapon, on a successful attack, the weapon deals one hit. The wielder may spend aa to gain an additional hit, and may do so a number of times equal to the weapon’s Linked rating. Additional hits from the Linked weapon may only be applied against the original target. Each hit deals the weapon’s base damage plus damage equal to the s scored on the check.

Nonlethal (Passive)

A weapon with this quality can only deal strain damage (damage applied to the target’s strain threshold). Because this is strain damage, not strain, it is still reduced by a target’s soak.

Pierce (Passive)

Any hits from this weapon ignore a number of points point of soak equal to the weapon’s Pierce rating. If the weapon has more ranks of Pierce than the target’s total soak, it completely ignores the target’s soak. For example, Pierce 3 against a soak of 2 ignores two points of soak, but the extra point of Pierce has no further effect.

Prepare (Passive)

Items with this quality require time to set up before being used. The user must perform a number of preparation maneuvers equal to the item’s Prepare rating before using the item (if the item is a weapon, “using” it would be making attacks with the weapon). At your GM’s discretion, moving with the item, being knocked prone with the item, or other disruptions may require the user to perform the preparation maneuvers again before using the item.

Slow-Firing (Passive)

Slow-Firing weapons tend to deal incredible damage, but need time to recharge or cool down between shots. A weapon’s Slow-Firing rating dictates the number of rounds that must pass before the weapon can be fired again after attacking. For example, a gauss rifle with Slow-Firing 2 must wait two rounds after being fired before it can be fired again.

Stun (Active)

A weapon with Stun can deal strain to the target. When the Stun quality is activated, it inflicts strain equal to the weapon’s Stun rating. Since this is strain, and not strain damage, it is not reduced by the target’s soak.

Sunder (Active)

When activating Sunder, the attacker chooses one item openly wielded by the target (such as a weapon, shield, or item on a belt). That item is damaged one step: to minor if undamaged, from minor to moderate, or from moderate to major. If an item already suffering major damage is the target of a successful Sunder, it is destroyed.

Sunder requires a to activate, and may be activated even if the attack is unsuccessful. Sunder may be activated multiple times in the same attack, but each activation must be applied to the same item, potentially taking it from undamaged to destroyed in a single attack.

Superior (Passive)

A Superior item is a sterling example of its kind, representing masterful craftsmanship. A Superior item generates automatic a on all checks related to its use. A Superior weapon has its base damage increased by one, while a Superior armor hase its encumbrance reduced by one and its soak value increased by one.

Throwable (Passive)

A melee weapon with the Throwable quality can be thrown at targets. Its range increases to Short range, and it gains the Limited Ammo 1 quality. Unless otherwise specified, weapons with the Throwable trait are thrown with the Ranged skill; lighter weapons, such as Knives use the Ranged (Light) skill, while bigger weapons, such as javelins, use the Ranged (Heavy) skill.

Unwieldy (Passive)

An Unwieldy weapon is a weapon that can be particularly awkward to use for those without impressive dexterity and hand-eye coordination. To wield an Unwieldy weapon properly, the character needs an Reflexes characteristic equal to or greater than the weapon’s Unwieldy rating. For each point of Reflexes by which the character is deficient, they must increase the difficulty of all checks made while using the weapon by one.

Vicious (Passive)

When an attack with this weapon results in a Critical Injury or Hit, the character adds ten times the Vicious rating to the Critical roll. With Vicious 3, for example, you would add +30 to the resulting Critical Injury or Hit result.

Not all goods are available on the open market; The jurisdictions of nations and corporations have a keen interest of keeping exceptionally effective tools to themselves. In the hands of unauthorized personnel or civilians, the purchase, use and carry of these items is considered illegal, and is being pursued by law enforcement and private security alike.

What is and isn’t restricted depends heavily on the jurisdiction a character might find herself in, and therefore is subject to the Gamemasters discretion. Buying or selling illegal items on the Black Market typically requires a Streetwise check. The difficulty of the check depends on the rarity of the item and may increased or decreased, depending of the state of the local law enforcement capabilities (as determined by the Gamemaster). A hhh or d on the check could mean that something has gone terribly wrong; the seller tries to rob her customers, or the runners find themselves surrounded by undercover cops.

Knife

From simple pocket knifes and switchblades to tempered KA-BAR-style fighting knifes, these practical little blades are as much street-weapons as they are weapons for close-quarters-combat in real warzones. They are often highly concealable, extremely lightweight, practical and come in an unfathomable amount of different styles. In a pinch - or for some even as a primary style - they can even be thrown, making them extremely versatile tool in combat.

Baton

Batons are clubs, made out of rubber, wood or flexible metals that are usually used by corporate security and police forces to brutalize anyone unfortunate enough to stand between them and the point they want to go. Even though the baton has a price tag attached to it, it is not overly difficult to find a wrench, a lead pipe or a pipe with a nail in it that does the job just as well. Some of them are even collapsible.

Stun Batons are standard riot-control weapons, often viewed as a symbol of corporate-government controlled police oppression, but still pragmatically useful to Shadowrunners. Stun Batons consist of a battery in the head of the weapon that discharges heavy electric shocks to anyone unfortunate enough to be hit with it. Usually, Stun Batons charge through induction and inform their users about their charge level.

Sword

This is a sharp, heavy piece of metal designed to cut their targets wide open. Available in a wide variety of styles (wakizashi, seax, scimitar, jian, machete, and so on and so forth), these one-handed blades are not as formidable as a katana in the hands of trained street samurais, but substantially easier to hide.

Tomahawk

These modern takes on classic Native American weapons give as much respect to their predecessors as they can while moving into the 21st century. Made from modern composites, balanced for throwing and optimized for maiming, these handy little things are popular among the urban tribal street gangs; especially the First Nations in Seattle, the Salish Rangers and the Sioux Wildcats are often very fond of these finely crafted killing tools.

Staff

These large, heavy sticks are popular with magicians and monk-styled adepts going for the traditional look. Available in composite, hardwood, high-carbon steel and other materials.

Katana

This isn’t your great-grandfathers traditionally made sword, chummer, but this doesn’t stop this weapon from being an extremely deadly tool in the right hands. Mostly popularized by trideos, the katana have become a cliched staple for the street samurai and shadowrunners in general. Everyone – including deckers and mages – carries one of these, which is why there are probably more katanas on the market than of any other weapon.

Katanas are rather heavy, and therefore wielded with both hands.

Polearm

Polearms include all weapons that are, at their very essence, blades at the end of a very long stick. A polearm usually features an axe-head, a glaive, a spear point or a similar maiming implement. Due to their length, polearms are almost impossible to conceal, but usually provide an edge against other melee weapons, making them a popular choice against trolls or other large critters if firearms aren't an option.

Combat Axe

This barbaric, two-handed tungsten-alloy monster may appear primitive, but can be even deadlier than a firearm in the hands of the heavily augmented cyber-monsters of the late 21st century. Available in single- or double-bladed styles. Go ahead, go medieval.

Heavy Pistol

The bigger, meaner brother of the pistol family. The heavy pistol utilizes bigger cartridges than its’ civilian counterparts (or even most SMGs, for that matter) and therefore pack a bigger punch than most other handguns. Shadowrunners, Corporate Agents and Drugged-Up Gang-Bangers alike favor these weapons as their main weapons in close quarters. Unlike their lighter counterparts, heavy pistols often require licenses and are not always completely legal to buy and carry.

Models Include: Colt Government 2066, Browning Ultra-Power, Ares Predator V

Holdout Pistol

Hold-out pistols are very cheap and concealable, making them perfect for sneaking into a meet or wearing as a back-up piece. Street-scum often carry these as a self-defense weapon. They don’t have much else going for them.

Models Include: Streetline Special, Fianchetti Tiffany Self-Defender 2075

Taser

This pacifists' weapon of choice fires small needles, that deliver a large electric shock through the attached wires into the unfortunate victim. Security forces, civilians and runners alike tend to carry these in order to non-lethally incapacitate their targets. Generally speaking, tasers are completely legal in almost all jurisdictions and surprisingly effective against armored targets, but require the re-cocking of the weapon after every shot. Some versions even come with inbuilt monitoring software to ensure that you do not accidentally kill your target, and get sued by their loved ones in the process. Just don’t expect gangers to carry these.

Models Include: Defiance EX-Shocker, Yamaha Pulsar, Tiffany-Defiance Protector

Mini-Crossbow

Originally developed as a weapon for silent assassinations, various models of dart-shooters and pistol crossbows have hit the market after the widespread success of the Ranger Sliver. Surprisingly, due to the simple construction of a crossbow, the mini-crossbow sees widespread use as a lightweight sidearm among scuba divers and subnautical combat divers.

Mini-Crossbows are silent, adding jjj to all Perception and Vigilance checks made to locate a concealed shooter. Mini-Crossbows also do not suffer penalties from being fired underwater, in zero-air- or zero-gravity environments. Mini-Crossbows must be reloaded after every shot, and can load a wide variety of arrowheads.

Models Include: Ares Silverlight II, Ranger Sliver Pistol Crossbow

Dart Pistol

Parashield and Narcoject have released a series of weapons that defined the industry strandard for non-lethal dart pistols. Usually carried as a specialized sidearm of special forces teams in hostage situations, dart pistols shoot injection darts that deliver their payload on successful impact. Usually, that payload consists of a strong narcotic agent, but these darts can be filled with all kinds of toxins and drugs, if one so desires.

Dart pistols can never deal more than one wound, and do never case any Critical Injuries by themselves; instead, when a successful ranged combat check with the dart pistol delivers a wound to the target, the dart administers its’ payload. The target then makes a Resilience check with a difficulty equal to the severity of the administered narcotic. By default, the darts inject narcoject or a similar nerve agent (see “Narcoject” (p. [Page]), but when a character is using a custom payload (see “Drugs and Toxins” (p. [Page]), use the table “Effects, Severity & Scarcity” (p. [Page]) to determine the relevant statistics for the specific narcotic.

Two-Handed ranged weapons often employ much more power and utility than their one-handed counterparts, but aren’t very subtile, so forget smuggling on of these into your fancy gala. Unless otherwise noted, two-handed ranged weapons use the Ranged (Heavy) skill.

Assault Cannon

These babies are the very definition of the word "overkill". Simply put, Assault Cannons are miniaturized versions of tank cannons, and provide enough firepower to plow through entire blocks of houses. While some of the cheaper models use an ungodly amount of propellant, higher-end models tend to be designed as railguns; they accelerate tungsten metal rods through the use of magnetic rails.

Models Include: Krime Cannon, Panther XXL, Ares Thunderstruck Gauss Rifle

Grenades

Grenades are explosive devices, usually intended to inflict damage in an area around their impact point or to smoke people from highly fortified positions. Grenades are usually thrown using the Ranged (Light) skill, but they can also be used with a Grenade Launcher, significantly increasing their range.

Grenades can be triggered in multiple ways: They can be triggered at impact, which causes them to explode immediately after contact with their opponents, they can be triggered with a time trigger, allowing them to bounce off surfaces before exploding after a set amount of time (usually a few seconds), they can be triggered using a signal through the Matrix, allowing them to be remote triggered after being placed on their intended spot, or they can be automatically triggered through visual or persona-scanning sensors, allowing them to act as a sort of selective mine. How a grenade is triggered is determined at the time of activation, and all grenades can easily be bought without any functional Matrix connection - preventing "unwanted accidents" by deckers.

Shock Grenade

These grenades, often nicknamed "stun grenades" or "flash-bangs" explode to create a bright, loud, shocking blast that is distributed equally over a large area.

Fragmentation Grenade

These classic killing devices distribute shrapnel in a large area after the initial blast, seriously injuring soft targets.

High-Explosive

HE-grenades are designed to deliver a rather small, concentrated explosion intended to blast through armor.

EMP Grenade

Electromagnetic pulse grenades are designed to be used against electronic equipment, specifically against communication devices, drones and vehicles, causing them to short-circuit and fail. Against metahumans EMP grenades are only really useful against targets possessing cybernetic augmentations, but they can really frag their lives up.

Smoke & Gas Grenade

These little tubes release a heavy cloud of smoke through vents in their hull that prevent anyone to see through them by visual means. Smoke Grenades are usually hot, preventing people with Therm-Optic vision to see through the thick smoke. The smoke generated by these grenades does not disperse easily, even outside, unless exposed to rather strong winds or heavy rainfalls.

After a Smoke grenade detonates, everything within Short range of the grenade becomes obscured by fog (meaning that a single Smoke grenade is enough to fill about entire room with smoke). The thrower of the grenade can choose to get inside of the smoke at the time of detonation. People from outside of the smoke suffer jjjj to all checks in order to perceive or target anything within or on the other side of the smoke. People caught within the smoke suffer the same penalty towards all people within and outside of the smoke, and must make an additional Average(kk) Resilience check at the beginning of each of their turns that they start in the smoke, or suffer two strain. This counts as poison.

Similar to smoke grenades, gas grenades do not disperse mostly harmless smoke, but instead disperse irritating or even harmful tear gasses and nerve agents. Gas grenades follow the same general rules as smoke grenades, but are generally see-through, and their effects are up to the toxin used in the grenade.

Smoke and Gas Grenades usually last for ten minutes, or an encounter. At the Gamemasters discretion, this time can be shortened in heavy winds or rains, or lengthened in especially stale conditions.

Advanced Safety Systems

This attachment can be applied to all ranged weapons (including grenades) and melee weapons that require a Matrix connection.

Base Modifier: The weapon cannot be turned on or fired unless the persona of the weapons' current holder is authorized by the owner of the weapon.

Modification Options: 1x Self-Destruction (if an unauthorized user tries to fire the weapon, it detonates, dealing one Critical Injury, and is then destroyed) Mod

Attachable Stock

This attachment can be applied to all guns and crossbows without the Cumbersome qualiy, except Holdout Pistols and Tasers.

Base Modifier: As a maneuver, the stock can be attached or detached from the weapon. When attached to a one-handed ranged weapon, the weapon can be fired with the Ranged (Heavy) skill, and increases their Encumbrance rating by 2. When detached from a two-handed ranged weapon, the weapon gains the Inaccurate 2 quality, but decreases its Encumbrance rating by 2. The stock itself has an Encumbrance rating of 2 while detached. Two-handed ranged weapons replace their pre-built stock with this, and do not increase their Encumbrance rating due to this attachment

Modification Options: 1x Folding Stock (Attach or Detach stock as an Incidental) Mod, 1x Lightweight (Enc. -1) Mod, 1x Quality (HP +1) Mod, 1x Quality (Accurate +1) Mod

Suppressor

As the classic assassin’s accessory, suppressors allow a user to keep unwanted attention from themselves by supressing noise. Unlike the silencers often found in mainstream action trideos, suppressors do not make a gun magically imperceptible; If a suppressed weapon is fired inside a small enclosed room, people will notice the gunfire. Rather, a suppressor will distort the sound of gunfire to a point that people will not perceive it as such, especially when the sound comes from behind a bunch of walls. This additionally hinders witnesses of an assassination in locating the shooter in time.

Due to this, after-market attachments of suppressors are generally illegal, but more often than not, corporate security – especially those within arcologies – carry suppressed weapons to deal with criminals without enticing mass panic within the closed environment. Guns sold with integrated suppressors are therefore known and registered, and – with the right licenses of course – legal to buy and carry.

Suppressors come in two variants: Attached and integrated. Attached suppressors do not cost Hard Points, but are rather big and clunky, and therefor add one point of Encumbrance to the weapon while attached. Attaching a suppressor uses a maneuver, and getting caught with one is generally a bad idea. Integrated suppressors are built into the gun barrel itself. They cost a single Hard Point, but do not add to the weapons bulk. Spotting an inbuilt suppressor requires a Hard (kkk) Perception or Mechanics check.

This attachment can be applied to pistols, submachine guns, assault rifles, precision rifles and sniper rifles.

Base Modifiers: Adds jj to all Perception or Vigilance checks made to locate a concealed shooter. Inbuild suppressors require a Hard (kkk) Perception or Mechanics check to be spotted.

Modification Options: 2x Improvement (Add additional j to Perception and Vigilance checks to spot shooter) Mod

Each bodytech entry has seven listed characteristics:

Name: Each piece of bodytech has a unique name, making it easier for you to find the augmentation.

Description: Each piece of bodytech provides a short description, and a detailed description; the short description in the tables below provide you with a short overview of the pieces general functions, while the detailed description provides you with a more in-depth description of it's functions, a list of available modifications, and general fluff.

Category: The category of an augmentation is a vague description of the overall type and location of the cybernetic. Usually, only one type of cybernetic can be installed per category, but this can be overruled (with the Gamemasters' permission). As a rule of thumb, bodytech generally replaces existing tissue, bones and muscle, so it doesn't make that much sense for a character to have two cybernetic spines, for example. Consider the description of the piece of 'ware when determining if and where a character can have it installed.

Essence: Each piece of 'ware has an Essence cost; when installing a piece of bodytech, this is the amount of Essence Loss a character suffers. Essence Loss is generally permanent, but uninstalling cybertech leaves an Essence Hole in the character, that can be filled with new cybertech. Most character have an Essence Loss Threshold of 7, and can only receive 6 Essence Loss without dying. Some bodytech causes more Essence Loss than others, and some list available modifications that cause additional Essence Loss, so keep that in mind when attending the next chop shop.

Limbs

Limb replacement bodytech unsurprisingly replaces a limb of the recipient. Most cyberlimbs are tailored to the general statue and muscular structure of their recipient and connect the users nerves to the sensor arrays fount inside the limbs, which allows them to used almost indistinguishable from their flesh-and-blood arms and legs. When bought off-the-shelf, cyberlimbs usually appear mostly metahuman-like to alleviate the effects of body dysmorphia, but owners of cyberlimbs across all social classes have adjusted their new appendages to their tastes; some colorful, some extremely lifelike and some so outlandish that it can barely even be considered a metahuman limb anymore.

Cyberlimbs are some of the more customizable pieces of gear in a runners’ arsenal, and therefore provide more room for attachments than any other piece of bodytech. Additionally, each cyberlimb increases the wound threshold of its recipient by 2 points.

Generally speaking, a metahuman cannot have more cybernetic limbs than they normally would have; The brain doesn’t really cope that well with additional appendages - at best, they would just flop around uncontrollably, and at worst they would overload any fry the users brain with too much unprocessable information. Additionally, with Essence cost and Hard Points in mind, each cyberlimb counts as a whole limb; a character with a single replacement hand for example would still treat the hand as a whole cybernetic arm, and can take all the modifications that a cybernetic arm cloud take (You and your Gamemaster should still come up with an explanation on why you manage to fit a whole grenade launcher in your fist though). Replacing a lost finger or toe does not require full cybersurgery, and while replacing these can come with a monetary cost, they do not cause essence loss.

Those who cannot afford any essence loss, such as awakened people, or people with incompatibilities to cybernetic implants in general, can use alternatives to full cybernetics; Limb replacements provide a clean and noninvasive, but flawed way to restore the functionality of a lost limb. These can range from crude hook hands and wooden stumps, to simple early 21st-style prosthetics.

More common, however, are modern limb-drones that are affixed to the users’ body and which can be controlled through the users’ DNI. These drone-limbs allow their users to be controlled similarly like their own appendages, and can even transmit some sensory input to the users brain, but do not have much room for customization, and are generally unfit for any task that require some degree of finesse; Any check made with that limb that require hand-to-eye-coordination, body control, or the feeling of touch adds jj.

Since these replacements are more akin to drones than to actual bodyparts, they cannot be customized like normal cyberlimbs, but they can take some modifications meant for smaller drones. Lastly, they can (quite literally) be disarmed; When any check involving the replaced appendage generates hhh or d, the limb detaches from the user. Weirdly enough, while detached, a drone-limb can be controlled like any other drone, albeit a non-autonomous drone due to it’s lack of a pilot program. Attaching and detaching a drone uses two turns worth of actions.
