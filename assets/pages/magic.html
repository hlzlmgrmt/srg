Magic works!

The Laws of Magic

R&D of multiple corporations and research in the field of Unified Magical Theory have found five limits to magic, that are, al least at the moment, impossible to breach:

Magic cannot influence the stream of time.
While some magicians can see glimpses of future or past events, they are powerless to influence them with time-manipulating magic. Additionally, as an apparent law of the universe, glimpses into the flow of time become less coherent and detailed the further they are away from the present, making them a way less useful prediction tool than most A.Is.

Magic cannot directly alter the fabric of space.
True teleportation, the folding of space and similar means of faster-than-light transportation is not possible with magic, and abilities that appear like instantaneous teleportation are generally just feats of superhuman speeds. Even though an astrally projecting magician or a spirit can appear anywhere they want, they cannot interact with anything in physical space, unless they anchor themselves to a vessel, and even then they are still anchored to their original plane of existence, and their physical bodies inside of that plane.

Magic cannot permanently create matter.
Magic can only form matter from one form to another, and conjure forth spirits in the form of sentient tools, arms and armor, but it cannot make these things out of nothing; if this would be possible, corporations would long have given up on ways to secure scarce resources.

Magic cannot return the dead to life.
There are magicians who claim that they can raise the dead. These magicians, most reknown of which are the Houngan and Mambo of the voodoo tradition, are guiding a spirit into the corpse of a recently deceased corpse, animating them in the process and giving them the appearance of returned life. While the voodoo-priests do not deny that they appease the loa in this way, some charlatans earn a fortune by conjuring forth illusions of their deceased loved ones. The sad truth is that, unless the deceased corpse is exceptionally fresh and in peak condition - about just outside of what a doctor with a defibrillator could do - magic cannot return those that have passed on. People have been saved while already standing on the rainbow bridge, and magic capable of doing that is exceptionally rare (and expensive), but when a person dies, their spirit vanishes; only echoes of these people exist, which necromancers sometimes use to create almost perfect copies of the deceased.

Magic cannot interact with the Matrix.

When designing magical elements of a story, a Gamemaster should keep in mind that breaking these limits is a mayor feat of magical prowess that would change the foundations of society by a large margin. Additionally, players should never be given the opportunity to break these laws, simply because the funding, research and resources needed to even attempt this far exceeds the capacity of even the most legendary Shadowrunner.

Spellcasting

Basics of Spellcasting

While each tradition has their own sets of believes, rituals and specifics about each and every spell, Unified Magical Theory has summarized most of the tried-and-tested spells into ten distinct spells:

Attack spells encompass spells that directly harm a target, such as Fireballs, Magic Missiles, and Stunbolts.

Augment spells embolden a target with magical energies, improving them or their gear physically and mentally.

Barriers conjure forth magical force fields and armor that keep harm from their targets, or serve as makeshift prisons.

Change spells manipulate living and unliving matter into new forms, allowing a spellcaster to bend solid stone, metal, and even their own bodies into new shapes and forms.

Curses inflict pain, afflictions, diseases and other deliberating effects to their unfortunate victims.

Heal spells encompass spells that can close wounds, alleviate diseases and allergies, and can even bring back the near-dead.

Influence spells invade the body and mind of their unfortunate victims, allowing a magician to control their actions, thoughts and behaviors.

Mask spells conjure forth multi-sensorial mirages that can entertain or fool anyone perceiving the illusion.

Move spells allow a magician to move and hurl objects and people, and allow the magician and their allies to levitate.

Sense spells allow a spellcaster to attune their senses to supernatural heights, gaining the ability to perceive distant places, read minds and gain glimpses into the near-future.

A spellcaster must learn every spell individually by spending Karma; either by taking a rank in the Expanded Grimoire talent, or by increasing their Spellcasting skill.

List of Spells

Each spell entry consists of six entries:

Name: The distinctive category of a spell.

Description: Each spell contains a short description, giving the magician some information about the general use cases of a spell, as well as limitations the spell has that perhaps other spells could accomplish instead. When using a spell to accomplish specific tasks, Players and Gamemasters should keep the intention of the spell in mind.

Sustained: Some spells need to be sustained by the spellcaster as an upkeep for their prolonged effects. In practice, this means that for as long as the caster and it's target are within the same scene, the caster commits l from their Spellcasting dice pool for each sustained spell. Each committed die removes a l from any magical skill check dice pool. If no l can be removed, the caster decreases the ability of the check once. A spellcaster can only sustain a number of spells equal to the total amount of l in their Spellcasting dice pool.
Only one of each spell can be sustained at the same time. For example, if a spellcaster wishes to cast Augment on multiple targets, they cannot sustain two Augment spells; instead, they can increase the difficulty of a single spell by adding the Additional Targets additional effect.
At the Gamemasters discretion, when a spellcaster is forced to maintain concentration, for example while suffering a Critical Injury, suddenly falling, experiencing mortal fear or similar trauma, they must succeed a Discipline check with a difficulty equal to the amount of spells they are sustaining, plus one.

Deactivate Adept Power: As an incidental, an adept can deactivate any amount of Adept Powers they have currently activated. Depending on the Adept Power, deactivating is releases all commited l used to activate it, freeing them up to be used for other powers the adept might want to activate.

Adept Powers

Each Adept Power conveys specific information:

Name: Like ordinary talents, each Adept Power has a unique name, making it easy for you to refer to the talent, and adding a bit of fluff.

Tier: Each Adept belongs to a tier. Since Adept Powers, like ordinary talents, count towards the talent pyramid, talents of Tier II or higher can only be taken if you own at least one more talent of the next lower Tier before buying the new talent.

Activation & Cost: Usually, Adept Powers have active effects that require them to either preemptively or reactively activate it. The activation of the talent usually come with a cost; when activating a power, the adept must commit an amount of l from their Qi-Magic dice pool equal to the listed cost. If the adept does not have any l to commit, they cannot use the power until they have deactivated other powers first. Some powers, like the Authority power, do not provide a cost. Usually, this is because the power boost other powers that require l to activate; in these cases, you do only pay the cost of the boosted power.

Prerequisites: Like ordinary talents, some Adept Powers required certain conditions before they can be bought and used.

Description: Each Adept Power provides a rule to their effect.

Metamagics

The term "Metamagic" describes a technique utilized by Awakened magicians, summoners and adepts which have attained a higher understanding of magic through initiation. Metamagics allow an Awakened to manipulate and utilize their magical powers in more powerful ways unmatched by their lesser peers.

In order ot gain a Metamagic, a character must gain a rank in the Magic Initiate talent; since the talent is a Tier 2 talent, and no ranked talent may be taken higher than 5, any magician can only gain a total of four different metamagics without the aid of specialized equipment, such as metamagic foci; an Awakened therefore should choose carefully which Metamagic suits their needs best. Almost all Metamagics utilize the Awakened character's ranks in Magic Initiate in some form, making already mastered metamagics stronger with each metamagic gained.
