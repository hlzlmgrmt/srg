A magician casting a Sense spell gains the ability to perceive their surrounding by supernatural means. This includes the simple sharpening of ones own senses, and extends to perceiving invisible things, seeing through illusions and solid objects, and even into reading minds. Some exceptionally powerful practitioners of Sensing magic are even said to be able to see glimpses of the future.

Sustained: Yes

Base Spell: You enhance your own senses. If successful, you upgrade the ability of all Perception checks you make once.

Base Difficulty: Easy(k)

Additional Effects:

Detect: When casting the spell, the character must choose a trigger, such as aggression, danger, magic or the presence of certain objects, critters or known individuals. When the character is within the same scene as their chosen trigger, they get informed about their presence.

Empowered: The caster can remove j equal to their ranks in Spellcasting to all Perception check they make. (This includes seeing in total darkness or perceiving invisible creatures). Probe: The caster can telepathically probe the mind of a target within range of the spell. The target is aware of the probing, though they may not know the source of the spell. The caster can increase the difficulty of this effect: When increased once, the caster can read the targets recent memories and known knowledge of the target, and if increased twice twice the character might even gain information only subconsciously known by the target, such as deep fears or suppressed memories.

Sharpshooter: When the target of the spell uses the Aim maneuver with a Ranged (Light) or Ranged (Heavy) weapon, Sense affects the next Ranged (Light) or Ranged (Heavy) combat check.
