Using h / d on Magic checks.
Cost Effect
h/d

Draining: You suffer an additional strain. (This may be taken multiple times)
Magical Blockade: Add j to all magical skill checks until the end of your next turn.

hh/d

Failed Timing: Your spell or conjuration goes into effect either at the start of the next round, or one minute later.
Magical Maelstrom: until the end of the next round, adversarial spellcasters and spirits add j to all Magical skill checks against your character.

hhh/d

Physical Drain: You suffer a Critical Injury.
Strain Implement: A magical implement you were using for the skill check gets damaged by one step.
Obvious Magic: Your attempt at using magic becomes spectacular, making everyone around you aware of your attempt to use magic.
Displeased Spirit: Your spirit appears unhappy to be summoned by your character, and only does your bidding because it is bound to do so. Downgrade the ability of all checks your spirit makes. At the Gamemasters discretion, an exceptionally unhappy spirit will do their tasks according to the letter, and either do the bare minimum of the task, or tries to actively get its master into trouble.

d

Stunted Magic: Until the end of the encounter or scene, count your Essence Loss as one higher than it actually is. If this would cause you to burn out, you instead take a Critical Injury and are unable to use magic until the end of the encounter or scene.
Angered Spirit: Your spirit is exceptionally angry that you have conjured it, let alone that you dare to command it like some lowly servant. You suffer a Critical Injury, and then must immediately succeed a contested Disciple vs. Discipline check against the spirit in order to remain in control of is actions, after which this effect is treated like the Displeased Spirit effect. If you fail, your conjured spirit immediately becomes hostile, and is added as a new separate adversary that only targets the conjurer. If the spirit is destroyed or banished, the conjurer permanently upgrades all checks attempting to summon that type of spirit again until they have made amends with it. (At the Gamemasters discretion)

dd

Overexert Essence: You suffer a Daunting(kkkkk) Critical Injury that inflicts one Essence Loss until healed. If this Essence Loss would cause your character to burn out, they burn out, even if they afterwards manage to heal the injury.
Overfocus: A magical Implement you were using is completely destroyed.
