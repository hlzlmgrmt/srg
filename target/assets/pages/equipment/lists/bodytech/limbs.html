Limb replacement bodytech unsurprisingly replaces a limb of the recipient. Most cyberlimbs are tailored to the general statue and muscular structure of their recipient and connect the users nerves to the sensor arrays fount inside the limbs, which allows them to used almost indistinguishable from their flesh-and-blood arms and legs. When bought off-the-shelf, cyberlimbs usually appear mostly metahuman-like to alleviate the effects of body dysmorphia, but owners of cyberlimbs across all social classes have adjusted their new appendages to their tastes; some colorful, some extremely lifelike and some so outlandish that it can barely even be considered a metahuman limb anymore.

Cyberlimbs are some of the more customizable pieces of gear in a runners’ arsenal, and therefore provide more room for attachments than any other piece of bodytech. Additionally, each cyberlimb increases the wound threshold of its recipient by 2 points.

Generally speaking, a metahuman cannot have more cybernetic limbs than they normally would have; The brain doesn’t really cope that well with additional appendages - at best, they would just flop around uncontrollably, and at worst they would overload any fry the users brain with too much unprocessable information. Additionally, with Essence cost and Hard Points in mind, each cyberlimb counts as a whole limb; a character with a single replacement hand for example would still treat the hand as a whole cybernetic arm, and can take all the modifications that a cybernetic arm cloud take (You and your Gamemaster should still come up with an explanation on why you manage to fit a whole grenade launcher in your fist though). Replacing a lost finger or toe does not require full cybersurgery, and while replacing these can come with a monetary cost, they do not cause essence loss.

Those who cannot afford any essence loss, such as awakened people, or people with incompatibilities to cybernetic implants in general, can use alternatives to full cybernetics; Limb replacements provide a clean and noninvasive, but flawed way to restore the functionality of a lost limb. These can range from crude hook hands and wooden stumps, to simple early 21st-style prosthetics.

More common, however, are modern limb-drones that are affixed to the users’ body and which can be controlled through the users’ DNI. These drone-limbs allow their users to be controlled similarly like their own appendages, and can even transmit some sensory input to the users brain, but do not have much room for customization, and are generally unfit for any task that require some degree of finesse; Any check made with that limb that require hand-to-eye-coordination, body control, or the feeling of touch adds jj.

Since these replacements are more akin to drones than to actual bodyparts, they cannot be customized like normal cyberlimbs, but they can take some modifications meant for smaller drones. Lastly, they can (quite literally) be disarmed; When any check involving the replaced appendage generates hhh or d, the limb detaches from the user. Weirdly enough, while detached, a drone-limb can be controlled like any other drone, albeit a non-autonomous drone due to it’s lack of a pilot program. Attaching and detaching a drone uses two turns worth of actions.
