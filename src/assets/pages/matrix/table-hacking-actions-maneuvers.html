Maneuvers in the Matrix
Maneuvers
Switch Interface Mode: Your character changes from AR to VR or vice versa.
Slot/Unslot/Change Cyberprogram: You can slot, change or unslot a cyberprogram to or from your Cyberdeck or RCC.
Mark: Until the end of the round, your next Hacking skill check adds j. You can use this maneuver multiple times per round, adding j each time.
Issue Command: You issue command to a drone, vehicle or program that you control. At the Gamemasters discretion, more complex commands may require a separate Operating check. If you hijack a device for more than one turn.
Zoom To Target (only in VR): Your character moves their persona to a target within Long range, reducing the range between them to Short range.
Actions
Data Spike: Your character attacks a persona, icon or device with malicious code. On a successful Hacking combat check, the target suffers an amount of System Strain equal to your character's Intellect rating, plus any uncancelled s. If the target does not nave a System Strain monitor, it instead gets damaged by one step, plus an additional step for every uncancelled ss, which can potentially break a device in one go.
Take Over: Your character hijacks a device. On a success, until the end the turn, you can use the device as if you were its' owner, plus an additional turn for each uncanceled s. As part of the hack, you can issue a simple command; your target acts immediately after your turn.
Jam Device: You cut off all direct communication from a device or persona. On a success, until the end of the turn, the target cannot send or receive any messages through the Matrix, plus an additional turn for each s generated on the hack.
Trace: You attempt to find hidden icons and personal. Make a Computers check. On a success, you find and trace a single icon or persona you were looking for, or the most dangerous or interesting icon or persona in the designated area.
Hide: Your character tries to hide icons or their persona from Matrix perception. Make an opposed Hacking vs. Computers skill check. On a success, your adversary loses track of your persona or your icons, and must spend an opposed Trace action against your Hacking skill to find you again. When you use any Hacking action while hiding, an actively searching adversary immediately spots you.
