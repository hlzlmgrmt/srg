Traditions

Example Traditions

The following contains a few example traditions that you can expect adversaries (or maybe yourself) to follow loosely. These entries contain the following information about a tradition:

Name: The distinctive name of the tradition.

Favored Characteristic: A follower of the tradition often has a characteristic that is higher than the rest of their characteristics. This is often the tradition chosen by the Magic Initiate talent.

Favored Skill: A follower of the tradition almost always has at least one or two ranks in a certain skill, initiates often prove to be event more skilled in this skill. This is often the skill chosen by the Magic Initiate (Improved) talent.

Favored Aspects: This entry describes what magical skills a follower of the tradition usually practices. While exceptions to this exist, they are rather uncommon.

Favored Spells, Spirits & Adept Powers: This entry describes the spells, spirits or adept powers a follower of the tradition usually employ, as well as formulae they usually know. This entry usually only describes entries for their favored aspects.

Favored Metamagics: This entry describes the metamagics an initiate of the tradition usually learns.

Metamagics

The term "Metamagic" describes a technique utilized by Awakened magicians, summoners and adepts which have attained a higher understanding of magic through initiation. Metamagics allow an Awakened to manipulate and utilize their magical powers in more powerful ways unmatched by their uninitiated peers.

Metamagics are - at their core - talents, which differ in two ways from ordinary magical talents. First, metamagic talents are limited for an individual Awakened. In order ot gain a Metamagic, a character must gain a rank in the Magic Initiate talent; since the talent is a Tier 2 talent, and no ranked talent may be taken higher than 5, any magician can only gain a total of four different metamagics without the aid of specialized equipment, such as metamagic foci. This makes metamagics a specialized skillset, that can be useful in differentiating traditions from each other.

Second, metamagics mainly utilize the Awakened character's ranks in Magic Initiate instead of skill ranks. This makes almost all metamagics independent from their actual skills, and makes each individual metamagic stronger with each additional metamagic gained.
