Tap into Qi: As a maneuver, an adept can commit one [[p]] from their Qi-Magic dice pool and choose a non-combat skill with the Brawn or Agility characteristic. While they commit this die, they add [[w]] to the chosen skill. [(w)] generated on skill checks may be spend to add [(s)] or [(a)] to the result of the check. [(b)] generated on the check may be used to suffer one strain, and then add [(s)] or [(a)] to the result of the check. Uncancelled [(h)] or [(d)] from checks boosted by [[w]] can be used to trigger effects from the table "Using [(h)] / [(d)] on Magic skill checks.". Unless otherwise specified, only a single [[p]] can be committed per skill, but multiple [[p]] can be committed to different skills simultaneously.

Use Adept Power: Adepts have access to unique, supernatural talents, called Adept Powers. These powers usually require the adept to commit [[p]] to use them. Adept powers count towards the talent pyramid; additionally to the usual restrictions on talents, adepts must at least possess a Qi-Magic rating equal to the desired talent.

Deactivate Adept Power: As an incidental, an adept can deactivate any amount of Adept Powers they have currently activated. Depending on the Adept Power, deactivating is releases all commited [[p]] used to activate it, freeing them up to be used for other powers the adept might want to activate.

Adept Powers

Each Adept Power conveys specific information:

Name: Like ordinary talents, each Adept Power has a unique name, making it easy for you to refer to the talent, and adding a bit of fluff.

Tier: Each Adept belongs to a tier. Since Adept Powers, like ordinary talents, count towards the talent pyramid, talents of Tier II or higher can only be taken if you own at least one more talent of the next lower Tier before buying the new talent.

Activation & Cost: Usually, Adept Powers have active effects that require them to either preemptively or reactively activate it. The activation of the talent usually come with a cost; when activating a power, the adept must commit an amount of [[p]] from their Qi-Magic dice pool equal to the listed cost. If the adept does not have any [[p]] to commit, they cannot use the power until they have deactivated other powers first. Some powers, like the Authority power, do not provide a cost. Usually, this is because the power boost other powers that require [[p]] to activate; in these cases, you do only pay the cost of the boosted power.

Prerequisites: Like ordinary talents, some Adept Powers required certain conditions before they can be bought and used.

Description: Each Adept Power provides a rule to their effect.
