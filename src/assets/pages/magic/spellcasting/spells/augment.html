Augment spells magically enhance characters or objects, imbuing swords with magical power or enhancing a characters reflexes. Augment spells generally only work on voluntary targets, and cannot be in detriment to the target. Objects augmented through magical means are usually simple objects, such as swords, or simple metal alloys; enhancing a gun or drone often proves much more difficult.

Sustained: Yes

Base Spell: You select a skill using the Brawn or Reflexes characteristic, and a voluntary target engaged with you. If successful, as long as the spell is sustained, the target adds [[b]] to all checks made with the chosen skill. Only a single Augment spell can be cast on a target at the same time.

Base Difficulty: Easy ([[d]])

Additional Effects:

Effect Diff. Mod.
Enchant: The spell affects a Melee weapon a character within the range of the spell is holding. While the spell is sustained, in addition to other effects, the weapon increases their damage by one, and reduces their Critical rating by one, to a minimum of 2. The augmented weapon becomes magical, and may ignore an adversaries immunity to non-magical attacks. +[[d]]
Mental: The spell can effect any non-magical skill. +[[d]]
Swift: Targets affected by the spell ignore the effects of difficult terrain, and they cannot be Immobilized while the spell is sustained. +[[d]]
Empower: The target of the spell can add an additional [[b]] to all skill checks with the chosen skill. Additionally, the spellcaster may spend [(ss)] generated on the check to increase the [[b]] added to the selected an additional time (this can be taken multiple times, requiring [(ss)] each time). This effect is still not cumulative with other sources of [[b]], and may still not exceed [[bbbb]]. Alternatively, if the Enchant additional effect is a part of the spell, instead of spending [(ss)] to add additional [[b]] to checks made with the augmented weapon, you instead can increase its damage and reduce its Critical rating by another point (to a minimum of 2). +[[dd]]
Haste: Targets affected by the spell can perform a second maneuver without spending strain (they may still perform only two maneuvers per turn). The caster can increase the difficulty of the spell an additional time; instead of a maneuver, targets affected by the spell can perform a second action at the end of a round. +[[dd]]
